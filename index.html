About

Konstantin Wandel is a research fellow at the University of Bielefeld, working at LeibnizCampus / SOEP-RegioHub / DIW. His work combines computational social science, political sociology, regional inequality, panel data, NLP, embeddings, and questions of institutional trust under algorithmic and media-technological change.

Research Interests

  • Computational social science
  • Political sociology
  • Regional inequality and social mobility
  • SOEP-based panel analysis
  • Geospatial social science
  • NLP, embeddings, semantic drift
  • Generative media and consensus reality
  • Institutional trust and algorithmic mediation

Projects

Academic projects

Multidimensionality in Meritocracy: Evidence from the German Socioeconomic Panel

Binary scales contrasting meritocracy with recognition of social closure are intuitively appealing but empirically weak. Evidence from the German Socio-Economic Panel (SOEP) suggests that these attributions are often orthogonal.

Plant-Based Diet Diffusion in German Households

Panel-based analysis of vegetarian and vegan dietary transitions, household composition, and intra-household diffusion dynamics using SOEP data. With S. Kühne and K. Ostermann.

Semantic Drift in Parliamentary Speech

Embedding-based analysis of political language, topic structures, rurality, regional imaginaries, and semantic movement in large parliamentary corpora. With L. Gawenda.

Who speaks for the left-behind? Framing of spatial inequalities in the House of Commons (1988–2019)

Analysis of how spatial and regional inequalities are framed in British parliamentary discourse over three decades. With Z. Chen and L. Gawenda.

SOEP-RegioHub projects

GeoLAB

Infrastructure and research work around regionalized SOEP analyses, geospatial social science, metadata discovery, and reproducible data access.

Metadata-to-RAG Infrastructure

Search and retrieval infrastructure for regionalized datasets, variable metadata, codebooks, and linked research documentation.

Tools

Parliamentary Speech Vector Bank

A retrieval tool built on dense vector embeddings of parliamentary corpora, enabling semantic search across large collections of political speech. Supports research into semantic drift, topic modelling, and regional political imaginaries.
