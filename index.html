About

Konstantin Wandel is a research fellow at the University of Bielefeld, working at LeibnizCampus / SOEP-RegioHub / DIW. His work combines computational social science, political sociology, regional inequality, panel data, NLP, embeddings, and questions of institutional trust under algorithmic and media-technological change.

Research Interests

  • Computational social science
  • Political sociology
  • Regional inequality and social mobility
  • SOEP-based panel analysis
  • Geospatial social science
  • NLP, embeddings, semantic drift
  • Generative media and consensus reality
  • Institutional trust and algorithmic mediation

Current Projects

GeoLAB

Infrastructure and research work around regionalized SOEP analyses, geospatial social science, metadata discovery, and reproducible data access.

Plant-Based Diet Diffusion in German Households

Panel-based analysis of vegetarian and vegan dietary transitions, household composition, and intra-household diffusion dynamics using SOEP data.

Semantic Drift in Parliamentary Speech

Embedding-based analysis of political language, topic structures, rurality, regional imaginaries, and semantic movement in large parliamentary corpora.

Metadata-to-RAG Infrastructure

Search and retrieval infrastructure for regionalized datasets, variable metadata, codebooks, and linked research documentation.

Multidimensionality in Meritocracy: Evidence from the German Socioeconomic Panel

Binary scales of meritocracy minus closure-recognition, while intuitively satisfying, lack empirical backing. Evidence from the German Socioeconomic Panel (SOEP) indicates that these ascriptions are often orthogonal.
